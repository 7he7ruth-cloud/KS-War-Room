03 Castle Opening Plan Critical
  1. 1
    CT2 marks position — 5 min before castle opens
    CT2 identifies and marks the tile that is exactly 1:45 walk from the center of the castle. Do this at least 5 minutes before the castle opens to avoid scrambling.
    • At 1 minute to open: teleport to that marked tile and hold
    • Confirm walk time with coordinator before CB starts
  2. 2
    Castle opens — CT2 calls rally immediately
    The instant castle opens, CT2 launches their rally. This is the very first action of the battle. Everyone else is rushing castle during this same window — CT2 is racing to beat them to the castle.
    • Optional: CT2 can start in Alliance 1 while the rally fills, then swap back to Alliance 2 after it lands
    • Rally must launch before anyone physically takes castle
  3. 3
    Everyone rushes castle
    All players (CT1, turret leads, floaters) rush castle immediately after it opens. Use offensive heroes and 50% march speed boosts. Priority is getting castle first — turret leads can rush then peel back.
  4. 4
    Turret leads peel back immediately
    Once castle is secured, turret leads rush back to their turrets and start rallies. Must hold at least 2 turrets before 550's first rally hits.
  5. 5
    Early lead objective
    Clean execution = ~10 min early lead with castle + 2 turrets. This buffer gives us room to absorb and adjust as the battle develops — it's not a guarantee, it's a margin for error.
04 Castle Mid Battle Plan Ongoing
  1. 1
    Maintain pressure on castle + turrets
    Keep structure. Turret callers apply pressure throughout — hold 2+ turrets for lethality bonus at all times. If 550 takes a turret, counter-rally it immediately to reclaim and maintain the bonus.
  2. 2
    Bounce 550 — exploit their timing gaps
    Prioritise TG5 troops. Exploit any 5+ second gap between 550's rallies by sending timed reinforcements. Someone in Alliance 1 must actively watch 550's rally screen and relay timing to the counter team in real time — so they know when to counter, when to pause, and when 550 is faking (calling rally, cancelling, waiting 10-30s to re-call).
    • Someone dedicated to reminding people in AC to time their reinforcements
    • Remind garrison players to hold the 60/40 ratio steady
  3. 3
    Counter rally — all counter teams hold until the watcher calls it
    CT2, CT3, and CT4 (if active) are all countering 550's rallies. All counter leads stage near castle simultaneously. Do NOT launch proactively. Wait for the player watching 550's rally screen in the main castle team to call out when 550 commits — then the coordinator fires the sequence, with each counter team launching in turn.
    • All counter teams (CT2 through CT3/CT4) are pre-staged and ready — this is a coordinated response, not just one team
    • Cancel and reset if timing is off — losing 20s beats a mistimed rally
    • Coordinator calls 5-second countdown: "5… 4… 3… 2… Player GO… count… Player GO"
  4. 4
    Castle swap after successful counter
    CT1 times their walk so they arrive 30 seconds after counter rally retakes castle — giving the counter team time to assess and fully exit. Entire garrison must walk at exactly the same time. Dedicated garrison exit coordinator kicks stragglers. CT1 counts down (20… 15… 10… 9… 8… 7… 6… 5… 4…) so everyone moves together.

09 Castle Swap — Ghost March Deception Active Tactic
  1. 1
    March skin swaps
    Occasionally change march skin appearance to buy 2–3 extra seconds while 550 processes what they're seeing. Small edge, easy to execute.
  2. 2
    Send → Recall → Resend fake pattern
    Use send/recall/resend cycles to further confuse 550 if they're watching closely. Not required every swap, but available as a tool when pressure is high.

10 Garrison Management — Mid-Battle Swap Protocol Coordination
This protocol covers every castle swap during the battle — not just the opening. Each time a counter rally succeeds and CT1 needs to take back the garrison, these steps apply.
  1. 1
    CT2 opens in Alliance 1 — swap is seamless
    CT2 starts in Alliance 1 alongside CT1 when castle opens, so the garrison fills naturally. After CT2's rally lands and castle is secured, CT2 swaps back to Alliance 2. Alliance 1 players hold garrison while CT1 walks to castle.
  2. 2
    Garrison begins emptying as CT1 walks
    As CT1 lead's march departs, garrison recalls — but designated stayers must remain behind. These stayers are on VC and know how to recall on command.
  3. 3
    Minimum 400k garrison at all times
    Designate 2–4 stayers. Ideal: one player with 20% deployment capacity boost ensures 400k+ even with minimal headcount. These players must be comfortable staying back during transitions and must not leave without coordinator approval.
  4. 4
    Coordinated walk — countdown enforced
    The entire garrison must walk at exactly the same time when CT1 arrives. Garrison Exit Coordinator calls the countdown: "20… 15… 10… 9… 8… 7… 6… 5… 4…" — everyone exits together on GO. No stragglers.
  5. 5
    Garrison Exit Coordinator kicks stragglers
    Dedicated role: one person watches garrison headcount, calls recalls on anyone slow to exit, and confirms the castle is clear for CT1 to land cleanly.
